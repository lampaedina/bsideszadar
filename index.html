What We’re Looking For

We want real, useful information—no fluff or sales pitches. We’re here to learn from each other, hear new ideas, and get real insights from all corners of the infosec world, including professionals, freelancers, government staff, law enforcement, and hackers of all types.

We’re all about being vendor-neutral. While we appreciate the role of businesses in infosec, this isn’t the place for sales talks. Show us what you know and what you’ve done without turning it into a commercial.

What We’re Not Looking For

Our goal is to foster a rich and engaging dialogue at the conference. To help us achieve this, we kindly ask that you steer away of the following:

Sales Pitches
‍We're looking for genuine knowledge sharing, not product promotions.
‍
Marketing Content
‍Keep it technical and focused on innovation rather than marketing.
‍
Fear Mongering
‍We value presentations that empower and educate, not those that use fear as a driver.
‍
Incomplete Proposals
Please make sure your proposal is complete and thought-out when you submit it.

We appreciate your understanding and cooperation in keeping the content valuable and relevant to all attendees.

About Zadar

The city of Zadar, situated on the beautiful Dalmatian coast of Croatia, is known for its rich history and cultural significance. It is the oldest continuously inhabited Croatian city and is well-known for its Roman and Venetian ruins, medieval churches, and cosmopolitan culture. Zadar's unique attractions include the Sea Organ and Sun Salutation, which are modern architectural marvels that interact with nature.

Zadar also serves as an important transport hub with excellent connections to the Croatian islands and the Adriatic Sea, making it a vibrant gateway for tourists exploring the region.

code of conduct

Chatham House Rule

The BSides Zadar conference is governed by the Chatham House Rule. This means that while attendees are free to use the information received, they must not reveal the identity or affiliation of any speaker, nor that of any other participant. Learn more about the Chatham House Rule here.

Be Excellent to Each Other

All attendees, speakers, sponsors, and volunteers at BSides Zadar must agree to and follow this code of conduct, which will be strictly enforced throughout the event. We expect everyone to help ensure a safe and welcoming environment for all.

Our Commitment

BSides Zadar is dedicated to providing a harassment-free conference experience for everyone, regardless of skill, age, gender, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, or religion. We do not tolerate harassment of conference participants in any form.

Recording and Photography

Recording or photographing the presentations, talks, and workshops is prohibited unless explicitly authorized by the organizer or the speaker. This policy is in place to respect the privacy and rights of our speakers and participants.

Inappropriate Behavior

Sexual language and imagery are not appropriate for any conference venue, including talks, workshops, parties, X/Twitter, and other online media. Harassment includes, but is not limited to:

Offensive verbal comments related to skill, age, gender, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, religion Sexual images in public spaces Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following Harassing photography or recording Sustained disruption of talks or other events Inappropriate physical contact Unwelcome sexual attention Participants asked to stop any harassing behavior are expected to comply immediately.

Sponsors and Exhibitors

Sponsors are also subject to the anti-harassment policy. In particular, sponsors must not use sexualized images, activities, or other materials. Booth staff (including volunteers) should not wear sexualized clothing/uniforms/costumes, or otherwise create a sexualized environment.

Enforcement

If a participant engages in harassing behavior, conference organizers may take any action they deem appropriate, including warning the offender or expelling them from the conference without a refund.

Reporting

If you are being harassed, notice that someone else is being harassed, or have any other concerns, please contact a member of the conference staff immediately.

Scope

We expect participants to follow these rules at all conference venues and conference-related social events, as well as on social media.

Assistance

Our team is eager to help you with any issues you might encounter. Feel free to reach out!

sponsors

BSides Zadar conference is a free event that brings together security professionals for discussions and demos. It’s an opportunity for the infosec community to connect and share ideas. Though there’s no cost to attend, organizing the event does have expenses. We count on sponsor support to help cover essentials like venue costs, t-shirts, stickers, badges, and refreshments. While the event is not designed for marketing, sponsors can engage in ways that add value for attendees.

Why Sponsor?  
Engage with a dynamic community in the cybersecurity field. Sponsoring the BSides Zadar conference places your brand in the midst of key discussions and developments, gaining visibility as participants engage with and discuss your contributions.

Nobody knows what the “next big thing” will be, but these events are community driven with presentations voted upon by the industry. There is no magic to how it works, but we believe that listening to the underground can help prepare you and help identify what the next big thing might be.
